Earthquake Damage Prediction (Nepal)

Predicting post-earthquake building damage using robust ML and nested cross-validation.

Home Projects Contact

Overview

This project develops a predictive model to classify the structural damage level of buildings after the Nepal earthquake. It leverages Random Forest and LightGBM, advanced preprocessing, and rigorous nested cross-validation to ensure reliable and generalizable results.

Approach

  • Task: Supervised classification of post-earthquake building damage.
  • Models: Random Forest (with Randomized Search) and LightGBM (optimized via Optuna).
  • Evaluation: Nested Cross-Validation 5×3:
    • 5 outer folds for unbiased performance estimation.
    • 3 inner folds for hyperparameter tuning.
  • Metric: Final model selected based on mean F1-micro score across 5 outer folds.

Why Nested Cross-Validation?

Nested CV cleanly separates hyperparameter optimization from model evaluation, reducing the risk of overfitting and ensuring a fair, unbiased estimate of real-world performance.
