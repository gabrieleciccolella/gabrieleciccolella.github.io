Earthquake Damage Prediction (Nepal)

Predicting post-earthquake building damage using robust ML and nested cross-validation.

Home Projects Contact

Overview

This project builds a predictive model to classify the damage level of buildings after an earthquake. It leverages Random Forest and LightGBM, advanced preprocessing, and rigorous nested cross-validation to ensure reliable, generalizable results.

Approach

  • Task: supervised classification of post-earthquake structural damage.
  • Models: Random Forest (with Randomized Search) and LightGBM (optimized via Optuna).
  • Evaluation: Nested Cross-Validation 5×3:
    • 5 outer folds to estimate generalization;
    • 3 inner folds to select the best hyperparameters.
  • Model selection: based on the mean F1-micro across the 5 outer folds.

Why Nested CV?

Nested cross-validation cleanly separates hyperparameter tuning from performance estimation, reducing overfitting risk during model selection and providing a more realistic measure of generalization.
