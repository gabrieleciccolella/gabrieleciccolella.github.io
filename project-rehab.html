Thesis Overview

This project was carried out as part of my Master’s thesis in Intelligent Systems Engineering. The work focused on the development and evaluation of various Machine Learning algorithms for the automatic recognition of rehabilitation exercises.

A set of physiotherapeutic exercises was selected in collaboration with medical professionals and performed by a large number of patients under controlled conditions. The recorded movements were processed to build a comprehensive dataset, which served as the foundation for training and validating multiple ML models.

The main objective was to evaluate the capability of different algorithms to accurately identify which exercise was being performed, ensuring both robustness and generalization across subjects.

Methodology

  • Dataset: Built from motion data collected from numerous rehabilitation sessions.
  • Features: Sensor-based spatial and temporal movement descriptors.
  • Preprocessing: Normalization, noise reduction, and feature scaling.
  • Models evaluated: LDA, Decision Tree, Logistic Regression, SVM, KNN, Random Forest, Gaussian NB, AdaBoost.
  • Evaluation metric: Accuracy (%), computed using consistent preprocessing and cross-validation splits.

Conclusions

The results demonstrate that ensemble methods and linear models perform particularly well for structured rehabilitation data. The Random Forest classifier achieved the best overall accuracy, proving highly effective in distinguishing among different exercise types.

This study highlights the potential of data-driven systems to support physiotherapists in monitoring rehabilitation progress and detecting execution errors in real time.
